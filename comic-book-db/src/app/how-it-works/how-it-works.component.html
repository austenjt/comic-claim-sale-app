What Is This Platform?

This is a claim sale platform — a private, invite-only storefront purpose-built for running claim sales online. It handles the full experience: browsing a catalogue, claiming items in real time, cart management, order review, fulfillment, and a permanent sales archive.

The platform is duplicatable for a one-time fee of $3,000. That covers a fully configured, ready-to-run copy deployed to your own Azure account — your own domain, your own data, completely independent of anyone else's instance.

Once deployed, your ongoing infrastructure costs are approximately $3/month for Azure usage and Namecheap DNS. Azure's serverless pricing means you only pay for what you use, and a typical claim sale operation stays well within free-tier limits for most of the month.

The $20/month AI subscription (Claude) is entirely optional — only needed when you want to make changes to the site. Cancel and resubscribe only when needed.

For Buyers

What Is a Claim Sale?

A Claim Sale is a first-come, first-served selling format that originated in comic book communities on social media. The seller lists comics at fixed prices and buyers race to claim individual books — no bidding, no auctions, no negotiating. The price is set, and the first person to claim a book gets it.

This site is a private, invite-only storefront built around that format. The seller loads the catalogue and sets prices. Approved buyers browse and claim. It's intentionally small and personal — you know exactly who you're buying from.

Claim sales move fast. If you see something you want, don't wait — someone else might grab it first. Once a book is claimed it's off the table until released back or the order is cancelled.

Getting Access

This shop is invite-style — you need an approved account before you can claim anything. Anyone can browse the catalogue, but only approved buyers see the Claim buttons.

  1. Request an account — fill out the registration form with your name, email, shipping address, phone number, and preferred payment method.
  2. Wait for approval — the seller reviews your request. Once approved, you'll receive a PIN directly from the seller. This PIN is your password — it's generated once and never stored in plain text.
  3. Log in — head to the login page, enter your email and PIN, and you're in. If you ever lose your PIN, contact the seller to have it reset.

The Dashboard

The Dashboard is the main catalogue — every comic currently available for sale shown as a card with cover image, title, series, grade, and price.

  • Available comics show a green Claim button if you're logged in as an approved buyer. Comics without a price set do not show a Claim button.
  • Claimed comics show a grey Claimed label — they're in someone else's active cart. If that order falls through, the book may become available again.
  • Comics in your cart show a green In Your Cart label.
  • Sets appear with an orange Set N badge. Clicking a set card opens the set's detail page.

Use the search bar to filter by title or series. Use the sort dropdown to order comics by Oldest First, Newest First, A–Z, Z–A, Priced First, Claimed First, or Bidding First. The filter buttons let you hide already-claimed items or hide unpriced items.

Sets

A Set is a bundle of comics sold together as a single unit — ideal for runs, lots, or curated groups the seller wants to keep together. You cannot buy individual books out of a set; it's all-or-nothing.

The Set Detail page shows:

  • A cover image and description for the set
  • The full list of member books with their individual grades and prices
  • The combined total price for the whole set
  • A Claim Set button (if you're an approved buyer and the set is available)

When you claim a set, every book in the bundle is added to your cart at once, each tagged with the orange Set N badge. Clicking Remove Set on any member book removes the entire set in one action — you cannot remove just one book from a set. All books in a set must be priced before the Claim Set button appears.

Bidding

Some comics are offered through a bidding format instead of a fixed-price claim. These are easy to spot — they show a Bid button once the seller opens bidding.

The format used here is a soft-close English auction:

  • Ascending price — each bid must be higher than the current high bid. The highest bidder when the timer expires wins.
  • Open bids — the full bid history (names, amounts, timestamps) is visible to everyone on the comic's detail page.
  • Inactivity close — there is no fixed end time. The countdown timer resets every time a new bid is placed. The auction only closes after the full window passes with no new bids — preventing last-second sniping.

How to participate

  1. Wait for the seller to open bidding — the Bid button on a comic only appears after the seller has opened that item for bidding. You'll see a live notification when bidding goes live.
  2. Place your first bid — click Bid to open the detail page and enter an amount. The first bid starts the countdown timer, which is visible to all buyers.
  3. Stay in the lead — if you get outbid, the live high bid and bidder name update on both the dashboard card and the detail page. Enter a higher amount and click Place Bid to retake the lead. Each bid resets the timer to the full window.
  4. Winning — when the timer runs out with no new bids, the comic is automatically added to the winner's cart at the final high-bid price. A notification announces the result to everyone on the dashboard.

Items won via bidding show a Won via bid tag in your cart and cannot be removed. Contact the seller directly if there is an issue. There is no minimum bid increment — any amount strictly above the current high bid is valid.

Comic Detail Pages

Click any comic card on the dashboard to open its detail page. Here you'll find:

  • High-resolution front and back cover images
  • Title, series, issue number, publisher, and publication date
  • Grading details — CGC/CBCS certification number and grade, or estimated grade for raw copies
  • Page quality, pedigree, restoration notes, and signatures if applicable
  • Sale price and any public notes from the seller
  • GoCollect and Grand Comics Database links if available

The Claim and Release buttons are also available on the detail page so you can review a book fully before adding it to your cart.

Your Cart

Your cart lives at My Cart in the navigation. Every book you claim goes here. The cart moves through a few stages:

  1. Open — freely add and remove comics. No time limit.
  2. Submitted (review window) — click Submit Order when you're done. This starts a short review period set by the seller. During this window you can still remove items but cannot add new ones. Any applicable discounts are calculated and shown here.
  3. Finalized — once the review window expires, your cart locks automatically. The seller will reach out to arrange payment and shipping.
  4. Fulfilled — once the seller marks your order as fulfilled, the cart closes. You can then start a new cart and claim again.

If a discount applies, you'll see a Discount line in your cart summary with the amount saved and a description of the rule applied. Discounts are recalculated automatically if you remove items from a submitted cart.

Set items are grouped with an orange Set N badge. The Remove Set button removes all books in the set at once — you cannot remove individual books from a set.

Orders

The Orders page shows your complete order history — every fulfilled order with its line items, prices, and totals. This history is permanently archived and will never be lost, even if the seller resets the catalogue between sale events.

Live Notifications

The app shows real-time toast notifications in the upper-right corner so you always know what's happening.

  • When you claim a comic: "Title #N" added to your cart — $XX
  • When someone else claims: "Title #N" added to UserName's cart — $XX
  • When the seller awards a comic: "Title #N" awarded to UserName — FREE!
  • When a comic is returned to sale: "Title #N" Returned to sale — useful for spotting books you missed coming back available

Each notification stays visible for 30 seconds and can be dismissed early. Multiple toasts can stack and clear automatically when you navigate to a new page.

Your Profile

Your Profile page lets you update your contact details at any time — name, shipping address, phone number, and payment notes. Keeping these current ensures the seller can reach you and knows how you want to pay when your order is finalized.

Payment

Payment is handled directly between you and the seller — there's no payment processor built into this site. Include your preferred payment method in the Payment Notes field of your profile (PayPal, Venmo, cash at pickup, etc.). The seller will contact you after your order is finalized to confirm the total and shipping details.

Contact

The Contact page is the best way to reach the seller outside of a formal order — ask about a listing, make an offer, or inquire about something not currently in the catalogue. You do not need to be logged in to use the contact form.

Admin Reference

Dashboard (Admin View)

When logged in as Admin, the dashboard shows additional controls on each comic card when Award Mode is enabled (toggled from the Sales page).

  • Award button — appears on any unclaimed comic. Opens a modal listing all approved users. Selecting a user immediately adds the comic to their cart at $0.00. If the user has no active cart, one is created. Already-claimed comics do not show the Award button.

Bidding (Admin)

Bidding is controlled at two levels: a global on/off switch and a per-comic opt-in.

  • Global switch — set the BIDDING_MODE environment variable to true on the Azure Function App to enable the bidding API. When false (or unset), all bid endpoints are disabled.
  • Per-comic opt-in — check the Enable Bid checkbox on a comic row in Comics Management. Only comics with this flag set can enter a bidding cycle.
  • Window length — set BIDDING_CYCLE (in minutes, default 10) to control the inactivity window. Each new bid resets the full window.

Opening and cancelling a cycle

Bid-enabled comics show a Start Bid button on the dashboard (admin only). Clicking it opens the item for bidding — buyers will see the Bid button appear and receive a live notification. Bidding does not officially start (the timer does not run) until the first buyer places a bid.

If no buyer has bid yet, a Cancel button appears next to the status label, letting you rescind the open offer. Once a buyer has placed a bid, the cycle cannot be cancelled — it must run to completion.

No manual action is needed to close a cycle. When the countdown timer hits zero, the browser automatically calls the finalize API, adds the comic to the winner's cart at the high-bid price, and notifies all online users. The comic then shows as claimed and is locked from further bidding.

Bid-won items carry a wonViaBid flag. They are priced at the winning bid amount, cannot be removed by the buyer, and are included in discount calculations when the order is submitted.

Manage Orders

The Manage Orders screen is split into three sections: Active Orders (submitted and finalized carts), Open Shopping Carts (carts with items that haven't been submitted yet), and Order Archive (permanently fulfilled orders).

  • Release — removes a standalone comic from a buyer's cart and returns it to sale. A "Returned to sale" toast is shown to all users in real time.
  • Release Set — appears on set member books. Removes the entire set from the buyer's cart in one action and returns all books to sale with a single notification.
  • UnSubmit — reverts a submitted order back to Open so the buyer can add or remove items. Useful when a buyer needs to make changes after submitting.
  • Mark as Fulfilled — only enabled once a cart reaches Finalized status. Uses a two-click confirmation. The second click permanently archives the order, stamps each comic with the buyer's name and sold date, and removes them from the dashboard.

Carts transition from Submitted to Finalized lazily — on the next page load after the review window expires. The window length is controlled by the FINALIZE_HOURS environment variable (default 20 hours).

The Order Archive section has two delete options:

  • Delete — removes only the archived order record. The comics remain in the database.
  • Full Delete — only appears on orders containing a set. Removes the archived order record and permanently deletes all comics in the associated set from the database.

Order Archive records are never touched by a database reset — the full sales history is always preserved.

Sales & Discounts

The Sales page lets you configure discount rules automatically applied when a buyer submits their order. Multiple active rules stack. Discounts are snapshotted at submit time, so historical totals are preserved even if you later change the rules.

Three discount types are supported:

  • Flat Percentage Off (RAW_PERCENTAGE) — deducts a fixed percentage from the cart subtotal. Example: 10% off everything.
  • Buy X Get One Free (BUY_X_GET_ONE_FREE) — for every group of X+1 books, the cheapest book in that group is free. Example: Buy 4, get the 5th free.
  • Percentage Per X Books (PERCENTAGE_PER_X_BOOKS) — for every X books, add a percentage discount (capped at 100%). Example: 5% off per 3 books means a 6-book cart gets 10% off, a 9-book cart gets 15%, and so on.

Awarded items ($0.00) are excluded from discount calculations. Each rule can be toggled active or inactive without deleting it.

This page also lets you toggle Award Mode — when enabled, an Award button appears on unclaimed comics on the dashboard.

Reset Database

At the bottom of the Sales page is a Reset Database section, intended for use between sale events. Type RESET to confirm. This will:

  • Archive any fulfilled carts not yet archived (order history is preserved)
  • Delete all active carts and discounts
  • Delete all comics and their associated images
  • Delete all sessions except your own (so you stay logged in)

The Order Archive is never touched by a reset.

User Admin

The User Admin screen shows pending account requests at the top and approved users in cards below.

  • Approve — approves a pending request, generates a PIN, and moves the user to the approved list. A modal displays the new PIN — copy it and send it directly to the buyer. It will not be shown again.
  • Reset PIN — generates a brand-new PIN for an existing user. The same modal appears with the new PIN. The old PIN stops working immediately.
  • Suspend / Reactivate — suspending a user blocks their login immediately without deleting their account. Reactivating restores access.

The admin account row does not show Reset PIN or Suspend controls — it displays Admin in the actions area instead.

Comics Management

The Comics Management screen is a full-featured data grid showing every comic in the database. Each row is directly editable — click any cell to update it and the change saves automatically.

  • For Sale — controls whether a comic appears on the buyer-facing dashboard. Unchecking it hides the comic immediately without deleting it.
  • Sale Price vs Target Price — Target Price is your internal reference; Sale Price is what buyers see on the dashboard and pay in their cart. Books without a Sale Price do not show a Claim button.
  • Date fields — Date Acquired, Published Date, and Date Sold use YYYY-MM-DD format. Setting a Date Sold automatically hides the comic from the dashboard.
  • Quick Add — use the Quick Add wizard at the top of the page to add a new comic with the most common fields in a two-step form.
  • Delete — the delete icon on the left permanently removes the comic, deletes all associated images, and releases it from any active carts.

Sets Management

  • Add Set — creates a new set container. Give it a title, series, cover image, and public notes. The container is priced at $0; the total shown to buyers is the sum of member books' sale prices.
  • Add to Set — assigns an existing standalone comic to a set. The comic becomes a member and no longer appears as a standalone item on the dashboard.
  • Delete Set — removes the set container and ungroups its members, making them standalone again. Blocked if any member is in an active cart or if the set appears in a fulfilled archived order (use Full Delete on that order instead).

Comic Detail Page (Admin View)

Admin detail pages include image management controls below the cover image.

  • Update Front Cover — uploads a new front cover image. Automatically resized if very large; a small thumbnail is generated for the dashboard.
  • Update Back Cover — uploads a back cover image. This section only appears after a front cover has been uploaded.

Uploaded images are stored with metadata (comic ID, title, series, issue number) attached to each image record.
