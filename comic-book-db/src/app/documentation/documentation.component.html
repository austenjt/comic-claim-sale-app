Documentation

Everything you need to know about browsing, claiming, and ordering.

Browse Comics
For Buyers

Getting Access

I am a private seller, selling from my personal collection, and so you'll need an approved account before you can claim anything. Anyone can browse the catalogue, but only approved buyers see the Claim button.

  1. Sign in — click Sign In and log in with your LightningComics.Rocks account. If this is your first visit, your account request will be sent to the seller for approval.
  2. Wait for approval — the seller reviews your request. Once approved, you'll receive an email confirmation. Sign in again to access the site.
  3. Fill out your profile — after logging in, visit your Profile page to add your shipping address, phone number, and preferred payment handles.

The Dashboard

The Dashboard is the main catalog — every comic currently available for sale shown as a card with cover image, title, series, grade, and price.

  • Available comics show a green Claim button if you're logged in as an approved buyer.
  • Claimed comics show a grey Claimed label — they're in someone else's active cart. If that order falls through, the book may become available again.
  • Comics in your cart show a green In Your Cart label.
  • Sets appear with an orange Set N badge. Clicking a set card opens the set's detail page.

Use the search bar to filter by title or series. Use the sort dropdown to order comics by Oldest First, Newest First, A–Z Sort, Z–A Sort, Highest Price, Lowest Price, or Claimed First. The dashboard filter buttons let you hide already-claimed items or hide unpriced items.

Sets

A Set is a bundle of comics sold together as a single unit — ideal for runs, lots, or curated groups the seller wants to keep together. You cannot buy individual books out of a set; it's all-or-nothing.

The Set Detail page shows:

  • A cover image and description for the set
  • The full list of member books with their individual grades and prices
  • The combined total price for the whole set
  • A Claim Set button (if you're an approved buyer and the set is available)

When you claim a set, every book in the bundle is added to your cart at once, each tagged with the orange Set N badge. You cannot remove individual books from a set; if you need to release a claimed set, contact the seller directly. All books in a set must be priced before the Claim Set button appears.

Comic Detail Pages

Click any comic card on the dashboard to open its detail page. Here you'll find:

  • High-resolution front and back cover images
  • Title, series, issue number, publisher, and publication date
  • Grading details — CGC/CBCS certification number and grade, or estimated grade for raw copies
  • Page quality, pedigree, restoration notes, and signatures if applicable
  • Sale price and any public notes from the seller
  • GoCollect and Grand Comics Database links if available

The Claim and Release buttons are also available on the detail page so you can review a book fully before adding it to your cart.

Your Cart

Your cart lives at My Cart in the navigation. Every book you claim goes here. The cart moves through a few stages:

  1. Open — freely add and remove comics. No time limit.
  2. Submitted (review window) — click Submit Order when you're done. This starts a short review period set by the seller. During this window you can still remove items but cannot add new ones. Any applicable discounts are calculated and shown here.
  3. Checkout — after submitting, three buttons appear: Shipping, Pay Now, and Pay Later. Start by clicking Shipping to enter your mailing address. Once your address is saved, the Shipping button turns grey and Pay Now / Pay Later become enabled. Click Pay Now to pay immediately with a credit or debit card via Square, or Pay Later to let the seller contact you about payment arrangements (Venmo, PayPal, etc.).
  4. Finalized — once the review window expires, your cart locks automatically. The checkout buttons remain available until payment is complete.
  5. Fulfilled — once the seller marks your order as fulfilled, the cart closes. You can then start a new cart and claim again.

If a discount applies, you'll see a Discount line in your cart summary with the amount saved and a description of the rule applied. Discounts are recalculated automatically if you remove items from a submitted cart.

Set items are grouped with an orange Set N badge. You cannot remove individual books from a set — contact the seller if you need to release a claimed set.

Orders

The Orders page shows your complete order history — every fulfilled order with its line items, prices, and totals. This history is permanently archived and will never be lost, even if the seller resets the catalogue between sale events.

Live Notifications

The app polls for activity every few seconds and records events in the Logs panel at the bottom of the dashboard. Click Logs in the footer to expand it.

  • When someone claims a comic: "Title #N" added to cart of user UserName — $XX
  • When the seller awards a comic: "Title #N" awarded to UserName — FREE!
  • When a comic is returned to sale: "Title #N" Returned to sale — useful for spotting books you missed coming back available

Log entries are timestamped and persist across page refreshes, so you can review recent activity even if you were away for a moment.

Your Profile

Your Profile page lets you update your contact details at any time — name, phone number, and payment handles (Venmo, PayPal, Cash App, eBay). You can also save a shipping address with structured fields: street address, optional apartment/suite, city, state, and ZIP. Keeping this current saves time at checkout — the Shipping modal pre-fills from your saved address so you only need to confirm it.

Payment

Two payment options are available after you save your shipping address:

  • Pay Now — pay immediately with a credit or debit card using the Square-powered checkout. Your card is processed securely and the seller is notified automatically once payment is confirmed.
  • Pay Later — choose this if you prefer to pay via Venmo, PayPal, Cash App, or another arrangement. The seller will reach out to confirm the total and coordinate payment directly. Add your preferred handles to your profile so the seller knows how to contact you.

Once payment is received, the Pay Now button changes to Paid ✓ and both payment buttons are disabled for the remainder of the order.

Contact

The Contact page is the best way to reach the seller outside of a formal order — ask about a listing, make an offer, or inquire about something not currently in the catalogue. You do not need to be logged in to use the contact form.

Admin Reference

Dashboard (Admin View)

When logged in as Admin, the dashboard shows additional controls on each comic card when Award Mode is enabled (toggled from the Sales page).

  • Award button — opens a modal listing all approved users. Selecting a user immediately adds the comic to their cart at $0.00. If the user has no active cart, one is created. The Award button is hidden when a comic is already claimed or has a CGC or CBCS grade.

Manage Orders

The Manage Orders screen is split into three sections: Active Orders (submitted and finalized carts), Open Shopping Carts (carts with items that haven't been submitted yet), and Order Archive (permanently fulfilled orders).

  • Release — removes a standalone comic from a buyer's cart and returns it to sale. A "Returned to sale" log entry is recorded in real time. Set items do not have a Release button; contact the buyer if a set needs to be released.
  • Shipping address — if the buyer entered a shipping address during checkout, it appears on the order card as a Ship to: block so you can prepare the shipment without contacting the buyer separately.
  • Payment status — a dropdown on each order lets you mark it as Unpaid or Paid. Marking a Submitted order as Paid also enables the Mark as Fulfilled button early (before the review window expires).
  • Admin notes — a private text area on each order for internal notes visible only to you. Saved independently of the order status.
  • UnSubmit — reverts a submitted order back to Open so the buyer can add or remove items. Useful when a buyer needs to make changes after submitting.
  • Mark as Fulfilled — enabled once a cart reaches Finalized status, or earlier if the order is still Submitted but payment has been marked as Paid. Uses a two-click confirmation. The second click permanently archives the order, stamps each comic with the buyer's name and sold date, and removes them from the dashboard.

Carts transition from Submitted to Finalized lazily — on the next page load after the review window expires. The window length is controlled by the FINALIZE_HOURS environment variable (default 20 hours).

The Order Archive section has two delete options:

  • Delete — removes only the archived order record. The comics remain in the database.
  • Full Delete — only appears on orders containing a set. Removes the archived order record and permanently deletes all comics in the associated set from the database.

Order Archive records are never touched by a database reset — the full sales history is always preserved.

Sales & Discounts

The Sales page lets you configure discount rules automatically applied when a buyer submits their order. Multiple active rules stack. Discounts are snapshotted at submit time, so historical totals are preserved even if you later change the rules.

Three discount types are supported:

  • Flat Percentage Off (RAW_PERCENTAGE) — deducts a fixed percentage from the cart subtotal. Example: 10% off everything.
  • Buy X Get One Free (BUY_X_GET_ONE_FREE) — for every group of X+1 books, the cheapest book in that group is free. Example: Buy 4, get the 5th free.
  • Percent Off Over X Books (PERCENT_OFF_OVER_X_BOOKS) — flat percentage off when the cart contains more than X books. Example: 10% off over 5 books means a 6-book cart gets 10% off; a 4-book cart gets nothing.

Awarded items ($0.00) are always excluded from discount calculations. Each rule can be toggled active or inactive without deleting it.

Each discount rule has an optional Exclude Sets flag. When checked, comics that are part of a set (i.e. claimed together as a group) are excluded from both the item count threshold and the discount savings for that rule. This lets you run a buy-X-get-one-free or percentage deal that only applies to individual single comics, not set bundles.

This page also lets you toggle Award Mode — when enabled, an Award button appears on unclaimed comics on the dashboard.

Reset Database

At the bottom of the Sales page is a Reset Database section, intended for use between sale events. Type RESET to confirm. This will:

  • Archive any fulfilled carts not yet archived (order history is preserved)
  • Delete all active carts and discounts
  • Delete all comics and their associated images
  • Delete all sessions except your own (so you stay logged in)

The Order Archive is never touched by a reset.

User Admin

The User Admin screen shows pending account requests at the top and approved users in cards below.

  • Approve — approves a pending request, generates a PIN, and moves the user to the approved list. A modal displays the new PIN — copy it and send it directly to the buyer. It will not be shown again.
  • Reset PIN — generates a brand-new PIN for an existing user. The same modal appears with the new PIN. The old PIN stops working immediately.
  • Suspend / Reactivate — suspending a user blocks their login immediately without deleting their account. Reactivating restores access.

The admin account row does not show Reset PIN or Suspend controls — it displays Admin in the actions area instead.

Comics Management

The Comics Management screen is a full-featured data grid showing every comic in the database. Each row is directly editable — click any cell to update it and the change saves automatically.

  • For Sale — controls whether a comic appears on the buyer-facing dashboard. Unchecking it hides the comic immediately without deleting it.
  • Sale Price vs Target Price — Target Price is your internal reference; Sale Price is what buyers see on the dashboard and pay in their cart. Books without a Sale Price do not show a Claim button.
  • Date fields — Date Acquired, Published Date, and Date Sold use YYYY-MM-DD format. Setting a Date Sold automatically hides the comic from the dashboard.
  • Quick Add — use the Quick Add wizard at the top of the page to add a new comic with the most common fields in a two-step form.
  • Delete — the delete icon on the left permanently removes the comic, deletes all associated images, and releases it from any active carts.

Sets Management

  • Add Set — creates a new set container. Give it a title, series, cover image, and public notes. The container is priced at $0; the total shown to buyers is the sum of member books' sale prices.
  • Add to Set — assigns an existing standalone comic to a set. The comic becomes a member and no longer appears as a standalone item on the dashboard.
  • Delete Set — removes the set container and ungroups its members, making them standalone again. Blocked if any member is in an active cart or if the set appears in a fulfilled archived order (use Full Delete on that order instead).

Comic Detail Page (Admin View)

Admin detail pages include image management controls below the cover image.

  • Update Front Cover — uploads a new front cover image. Automatically resized if very large; a small thumbnail is generated for the dashboard.
  • Update Back Cover — uploads a back cover image. This section only appears after a front cover has been uploaded.

Uploaded images are stored with metadata (comic ID, title, series, issue number) attached to each image record.
